The Monty Hall Problem
7/20/2018
The Monty Hall Problem is a classic example of an easy to understand problem that leads to an unintuitive result. Often, though, the solution to it is either presented matter-of-factly without addressing heuristic concerns or the problem is expanded to hit intuition without leading to an actual solution. An explanation that both hits at an intuitive heuristic that leads to a rigorous proof is presented.
